BACK

- D (Vertical Travel Distance of the Load): This indicates how far the load is lifted or lowered, calculated as D = h2 - h1, where h1 is the initial height of the load and h2 is the final height. Measuring the difference between the ending height and the starting height of the load's position (high - low) helps determine this value. Minimizing this distance can help prevent overexertion.
